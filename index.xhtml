Research

Keywords: algorithm design ; parameterized complexity ; scheduling ; graph theory.

I work on the design of efficient algorithms for scheduling problems. I am especially interested in characterizing the computational complexity of these problems, taking inspiration from the well established parameterized approach in graph theory.

Activities

Full list with abstracts. | [FR] Liste complète avec résumés.

  • Starting in May 2026, I will be a postdoc in the GROG team of G-SCOP at Grenoble INP under the supervision of Margaux Nattaf, Marie-Laure Espinouse and Pierre Xavier Thivel. I will be studying the scheduling of charging a fleet of electric vehicules, taking into account battery life.

  • From November 2024 to April 2026 I have been a postdoc in the ROMA team of LIP at ENS Lyon under the supervision of Frédéric Vivien and Anne Benoit. I have been studying the scheduling of radio-astronomical observations.

  • From October 2021 to October 2024 I was a PhD student in the RO team of LIP6 at Sorbonne Université under the supervision of Claire Hanen. I studied the parameterized complexity of the Resource Constrained Project Scheduling Problem (RCPSP) and its subproblems, possibly augmented with precedence delays and time window constraints.

    • Along with Armand Gissler, I received the PGMO Ph.D. Award 2025.
